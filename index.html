Modeling the Growth of Harumanis Mangoes (Mangifera indica L. cv. Harumanis)

In Perlis, Malaysia, managing the precise maturation curve of Harumanis mangoes dictates harvest logistics, grading quality, and premium pricing. Using a logistic growth model, we map the cumulative fruit weight $f(x)$ over 120 Days After Anthesis (DAA). Crucially, the derivative $f'(x)$ acts as our bioscience radar—measuring the instantaneous daily growth rate. This enables agrotechnologists to fine-tune smart greenhouse irrigation schedules and predict maximum nutrient assimilation phases.

Theoretical Framework & Math Formulas

Biological organisms grow sigmoidally. Rapid early cell division transitions into linear cell expansion, slowing down during biochemical maturation. We represent cumulative weight using the Logistic Growth Formula:

Cumulative Mango Mass $f(x)$
Yields total mass in grams ($g$) at $x$ DAA
Daily Growth Velocity $f'(x)$
Yields daily rate of change in grams/day ($g/d$)

Parameter Dictionary

$L$ (Carrying Capacity):

Maximum physiological weight of fruit at harvest (typically 350g – 500g for Harumanis).

$k$ (Logistic Coefficient):

Growth velocity index. Represents cellular metabolic rate & expansion speed.

$x_0$ (Inflection/Midpoint):

The DAA day where growth speed is maximized. Point of transition from acceleration to deceleration.
